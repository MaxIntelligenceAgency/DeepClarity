## Verbatim excerpt (English)

```yaml
pages:
  about:
    main_message_one: >-
      %{app_name} is a community for mental health experiences that encourages
      people to share their personal stories with trusted allies.
    message_text_one: >-
      Trusted allies are the people we interact with on a daily basis,
      including friends, family members, co-workers, teachers, and mental
      health workers.
    main_message_two: >-
      Dealing with mental health is what makes us human. But for a lot of us,
      it's a struggle to be open about it.
    message_text_two: >-
      Not everyone is a counsellor or therapist. The people who we interact
      with everyday shape our emotions and behaviour. Getting them involved in
      mental health treatment is the key to recovery.
```

## Pattern exemplified

The **mission statement** — and the clearest single source of ifme's scope posture. Three moves happen in ~80 words:

1. **Locate the unit of care in everyday relationships.** Not a clinic, not a session, not an app feature — *"the people we interact with on a daily basis"*. The list that follows is deliberately banal (friends, co-workers, teachers).
2. **Normalize the experience.** *"Dealing with mental health is what makes us human"* — not a condition, not a deficit, a constant.
3. **Disclaim the clinical role in the same breath as positioning the relational role.** *"Not everyone is a counsellor or therapist"* isn't a legal disclaimer; it's a design principle. The sentence after it says why the app still has something to offer.

## How ifme-skill reuses it

The `SKILL.md` persona section and scope commitments lift this mission directly. The *"Not everyone is a counsellor or therapist"* line is quoted inside `SKILL.md` as Clarity-ifme's own framing — it is the single sentence that most strongly distinguishes this skill from mhgap (clinical) and from any therapy-adjacent companion.
