## Pattern exemplified

The **content-teaching beat**: a small piece of framing delivered in plain language. Two things happen in this three-sentence chunk. First, the psychological idea is surfaced obliquely, through a universal observation ("our minds set in the future or dwelling on past experiences") rather than a clinical term. Second, the technical vocabulary — "present-moment-awareness" and "observing stance" — is introduced in the same breath that defines it by function rather than definition. No jargon is left standing without a context.

## How Clarity reuses it

The first beat of Clarity's three-beat arc is this move. Teach something small; name the phenomenon; do it in the everyday voice of the excerpt above. Clarity additionally **attaches the named tradition** (e.g., mindfulness / Kabat-Zinn, or ACT / Hayes) in this beat, where 29k's source did not — this is a deliberate upgrade documented in `SKILL.md § Upgrade rules`.
