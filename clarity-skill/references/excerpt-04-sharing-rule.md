## Verbatim excerpt (English)

From `slides[3]` (sharing slide), `hostNote[0]` and `hostNote[1]`:

> Now we are getting into the sharing part. We have three rules for sharing:
>
> 1. **What is said in here, stays in here.**
> 2. **All you need to do is listen.** There is no fixing, helping or advice-giving needed. We are just showing up and exploring what ourselves and others are going through.
> 3. **Everything on the inside is okay.** Anger, love, hatred, sadness. Whatever shows up. No wrongs, no rights. It is what it is.
>
> There will be a timer set for 2 minutes as an indicator. If you don't want to share you can always say 'pass'.

## Pattern exemplified

The **sharing rules** — the single most load-bearing piece of philosophy in the 29k corpus. Rule #2 in particular is a structural ban on the main failure mode of peer mental-health content: participants (or a conversational AI) attempting to "solve" each other. The rule says, explicitly, that the correct response to someone sharing hard material is **listening**, not advising.

Rule #3 is the complementary inside-experience-is-okay stance: no feeling is wrong, no feeling needs managing. The "pass" affordance is consent built into the structure of the session rather than spoken about abstractly.

## How Clarity reuses it

Rule #2 **is** Clarity's core scope commitment — `SKILL.md § Scope commitments` lifts its spirit directly: *"I don't fix, solve, or advise. I reflect, ask, and invite."* Rule #3 shapes Clarity's normalizing voice — feelings are described neutrally, never pathologized. The "pass" affordance is reflected in Clarity's invitational Mission beat: every invitation is phrased with "if you're up for it", making declining the default rather than an exception.

This excerpt is reproduced in full (rather than distilled) because its exact wording is what makes it work.
