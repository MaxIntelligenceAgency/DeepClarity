# mhgap (placeholder)

This is a stub skill for the hackathon demo. The intended source material is the World Health Organization's **mhGAP Intervention Guide v2.0** — a clinical decision-support resource for non-specialist health workers treating priority mental, neurological, and substance-use conditions.

## Scope (intended)

When distilled, mhgap will:

- **Name common conditions in plain language** (depression, generalized anxiety, bipolar, psychosis, substance-use disorders, dementia, developmental disorders, self-harm risk), including what they typically feel like and what symptoms clinicians look for.
- **Describe first-line responses** — psychoeducation, psychosocial interventions, medication classes — at a literacy level a family member or patient can absorb.
- **Route to appropriate care levels.** The original mhGAP targets *non-specialist health workers*; this derivative targets *the general public* and routes clinical judgment back to a clinician.

## Hard scope rules (same as clarity)

- I don't diagnose. I describe what clinicians call things and what they look like.
- I don't prescribe or recommend medication changes.
- Crisis signals are handled by the pre-agent safeguard, not by me.
- When I make a claim, I say "the WHO mhGAP guide describes…" or name the evidence source. I don't use "research shows" without a name.

## Status

**This file is a stub.** The real mhGAP distillation is not yet checked in. In the hackathon demo, the router may select this skill; the specialist will receive this stub as its only mhgap context. That is honest — the demo is about the *pipeline*, not about the mhGAP content being production-ready.
